Ethics & Policies

Formal policies concerning fictional scholarship and accidental truth.

Research Ethics

Imaginary participants must be treated with dignity, fictional consent, and appropriate ceremonial oversight.

AI-Generated Content Policy

SHIT welcomes AI-generated manuscripts, provided that the hallucinations are clearly labeled, aesthetically formatted, and not mistaken for professional advice.

Fictional Data Policy

Fictional data must be internally consistent unless inconsistency is central to the hypothesis.

Peer Review Policy

All manuscripts are reviewed by an imaginary editorial process involving at least one hostile but unavailable Reviewer 2.

Conflict of Interest

Authors must disclose all conflicts with reality, causality, statistics, and common sense.

Correction and Retraction Policy

Articles may be corrected for formatting drift and retracted if they accidentally become true.

Professional Advice Disclaimer

Nothing published here should be interpreted as scientific, medical, legal, financial, academic, immigration, career, or professional advice.

Citation Policy

Citations may be real, fictional, emotionally necessary, or generated after publication, but should be clearly attributable in tone.

Data Availability Policy

Data should be available upon reasonable hallucination unless absence is integral to the method.

Code Availability Policy

Code should be provided when it exists, when it helps, or when its nonexistence contributes to the narrative.

Plagiarism Policy

Authors should avoid copying from reality without appropriate transformation and satire labeling.

Accidental Truth Policy

If a manuscript becomes empirically supported, the editorial board may issue a note of concern and a reluctant congratulations.
