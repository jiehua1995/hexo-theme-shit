For Authors

Author guidance for manuscripts of unreasonable confidence.

Manuscript Categories

Retraction-Ready Report

For studies whose conclusions are mature enough for immediate concern.

Imaginary Dataset

For datasets that would have been useful if they existed.

Unnecessary Methods

For methods solving problems nobody was willing to define.

For simulations performed primarily in the author's imagination.

For dramatized academic suffering involving Reviewer 2.

For administrative documents with excessive confidence and insufficient budget.

For short claims urgently requiring references, preferably after publication.

Synthetic Review

For AI-generated reviews with strong formatting and uncertain ontology.

General Requirements

  • The title must sound more confident than the evidence.
  • The abstract should include at least one sentence beginning with "Here we show".
  • Methods must be sufficiently detailed to appear reproducible.
  • Results may be statistically significant, spiritually significant, or administratively significant.
  • At least one limitation must be acknowledged and immediately ignored.
  • References may be real, fictional, emotionally necessary, or generated upon reasonable hallucination.
  • Conclusions should exceed the available evidence by at least 300%.

Submission Checklist

  • The manuscript contains at least one unnecessary acronym.
  • The conclusions exceed the data.
  • Reviewer 2 has been emotionally anticipated.
  • All imaginary participants have provided imaginary consent.
  • The data are available upon reasonable hallucination.
  • The article is clearly labeled as satire, fiction, AI-generated, or not professional advice where appropriate.
