The ultimate disclaimer as philosophical statement

Abstract

Here we perform an existential reading of the phrase "further research is needed," the most common and most honest sentence in academic writing, which simultaneously admits intellectual humility and guarantees that nobody will ever finish the research.

Keywords: further research, philosophy, academic conclusions

Introduction

“Futher research is needed” is the most universally present sentence in academic writing. It appears in approximately 94% of conclusions. It is also the most honest sentence in the literature: a clear-eyed acknowledgment that the current work is insufficient, provisional, and merely the beginning of an infinite regress of inquiry.

Existential Readings

The phrase admits multiple existential interpretations:

  1. The Humble Reading: “We did some work, but there is more to do, and we acknowledge the limits of our own contribution.”
  2. The Evasive Reading: “We found nothing decisive, but saying so directly would be unprofessional.”
  3. The Strategic Reading: “We have created a justification for our next 5 years of funding.”
  4. The Philosophical Reading: “All knowledge is provisional. This paper is no exception. This sentence is no exception.”

The Infinite Regress

Every instance of “further research is needed” generates a new research program, which generates more papers, each containing its own “further research is needed.” This creates an infinite regress of academic inquiry, wherein each paper is both an answer and a question, both a conclusion and a beginning.

Discussion

The ubiquity of “further research is needed” suggests that the primary function of academic papers is not to answer questions but to generate more questions that can be answered by future papers. Science, therefore, is not a progression toward truth but an infinite machine for producing the phrase “further research is needed.”
