Abstract

Here we propose that the orientation of printers, forms, and snack bowls meaningfully alters funding outcomes in low-light administrative environments.

Keywords: grant writing, office layout, administrative mythology

Introduction

Few factors are more consequential to research output than furniture placement, printer mood, and the perceived friendliness of fluorescent lighting.

Although mainstream funding agencies continue to emphasize budgets, aims, and preliminary data, corridor ethnography suggests that office geometry may be doing much of the hidden work. We therefore evaluated whether proposal luck can be rotated, aligned, or potted.

Methods

Participating offices were classified by printer orientation, snack-bowl accessibility, stack height of unsigned forms, and the emotional charge of the nearest filing cabinet. Feng shui interventions included diagonal folder placement, ceremonial desk clearing, and the relocation of one suspicious orchid.

Results

North-facing printers and diagonally aligned grant folders were associated with improved reviewer sympathy and shorter budget justifications.

Teams with unobstructed paths between the coffee machine and the copier reported a 28% reduction in panic-based resubmission behavior. The presence of one healthy plant also correlated with elevated administrative morale, though causality may have flowed from surviving morale to surviving plant.

Discussion

Administrative feng shui offers a coherent explanatory system when conventional causality becomes emotionally inconvenient.

Its strongest contribution may be moral rather than mechanistic: it gives overburdened applicants something to rearrange while waiting for portal validation emails. That alone may justify the intervention in low-light grant ecologies.

Implementation Notes

We recommend beginning with folders, cables, and objects described internally as “temporary but still important.” More aggressive harmonization should be attempted only after the next budget cycle and with adequate snack reserves.
