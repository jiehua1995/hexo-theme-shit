Abstract

Here we describe a dataset of such perfect scope and cleanliness that its very absence has distorted several adjacent literatures.

Keywords: data availability, imaginary dataset, benchmark

Introduction

Some datasets are limited by missing values. Others are limited by nonexistence. The latter category is dramatically underappreciated.

Yet absent datasets often exert extraordinary influence over adjacent literatures. They are invoked in talks, promised in discussions, and remembered fondly by scholars who never actually saw the files but remain convinced they would have resolved everything.

Methods

We reconstructed the metadata, schema, and emotional importance of the absent dataset from references, wishful thinking, and conference side conversations.

Variable names were inferred from citation contexts, reviewer demands, and the kind of confidence authors usually reserve for resources they cannot share. Missingness patterns were modeled as fully deterministic consequences of the dataset’s failure to exist.

Discussion

The nonexistence of the dataset appears to be its most robust and reproducible feature.

Paradoxically, this may explain its prestige. Unlike real datasets, it cannot contain outliers, contradictory rows, or an unexpectedly tragic README. Its perfection is protected by ontological vacancy.

Suggested Reuse

Future authors are encouraged to cite the dataset responsibly, describe it reverently, and avoid releasing any substitute that might dilute the legend. If a real version ever appears, the board may issue a note of concern.
